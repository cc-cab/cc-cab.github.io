Corporation Adjudication Bureau

"Seek truth from facts"
The Corporation Adjudication Bureau is responsible for setting moral and legal standards within the corporation. It is to be noted that CAB members usually do not directly confront or deal with rulebreakers, that's a duty of the Civil Protection Branch. You could compare it to the legal system commonly used within some countries, with CAB being the courts, and CPB being the police...

The Court

Every violation against the Corporation Law first goes to the Civil Protection Branch, who shall gather any additional evidence, and write up an official incident report that would include a court date & time. Copies of this report would be then forwarded to the Corporation Adjudication Bureau, and the defendant. If the defendant may not appear at the specified time, they may appoint another individual to represent them, or write a letter summing up their defense. This will be the only chance to defend. A judge will then go over the report, might talk with any witnesses (if they can't appear, it goes the same as with the defendant), and issue a judgment. This judgment may be appealed (Magistrate > Judge > PM Director). Any judgment by the CAB Personnel Management Director may be sent back to them for further review, however, an appeal might not be possible.

Traffic Points

Every time an officer submits a traffic violation in the CAD, they are supposed to fill out a form (if they actually do it, that's a different story...) maintained by the District Records Office. Their inputs are then reviewed, and traffic points are then automatically shown in our database. If you reach over 5 points, your driver license will be suspended. If you reach over 5 points without having an active license (yes, it happens..), you will be blacklisted from getting one. Suspensions and blacklists are issued for an indefinite amount of time until they are appealed. During the appeal, a judge will choose when shall the punishment end.

Businesses

In order to do certain activities, you may need a license. This is defined within the business regulations. Getting a business license will not only grant you a permit to conduct the activities you desire, but also allow you to act as an organization - that, however, comes up with some responsibility. If you have an active business license, your business may be subject to frequent mandatory inspections to make sure you are following all applicable regulations for your field of operation - for example, if you were to be operating a business with firearms, the DRO inspectors would make sure that you are selling only products that have been pre-registered with the state. All business matters are usually handled through tickets. The public may view the history of conduct and inspections or other records of every business, even if they no longer hold their business license.
